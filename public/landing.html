What do I need to define to begin creating my protagonist's arc?
The Director
To begin creating Saeya's arc, you need to establish the gap between who she is at the beginning and who she must become to achieve her goal.

Since her main conflict is against the Society—which opposes her desire to listen and share music freely—her arc will be deeply tied to how she responds to that oppression.

We need to define three foundational pillars: The Starting Point (The Lie), The Catalyst (The Push), and The Want vs. The Need.

The Bard

The emotional journey is the strongest point of the text. The imagery is consistently vivid. However, the abrupt tonal shift between the prologue and the first chapter is a bold leap that can be disorienting.
